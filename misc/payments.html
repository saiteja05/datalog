1980s: Fixed Field Messages

Static payment formats with 20-year change cycles and zero innovation. Each character position was rigidly defined, making adaptation nearly impossible. Banks operated in isolation with proprietary formats that stifled cross-border interoperability.

1990s: Relational Messages

Change became possible, but each bank maintained its own schema and concept. The reasons for original designs were lost in time and complexity, creating technical debt that strangulated modernization efforts across the industry.

2018: ISO 20022 & JSON

Readable, flexible, and dynamically adjustable for ever-changing requirements. New capabilities can be added without impact to others. JSON (the native format of MongoDB) became the standard for payments data, enabling Open Banking APIs and modern architectures.

2022+: Data Monetization & AI

Value-add services enabling real-time analytics and immediate engagement with consumers. AI-powered enrichment, fraud prevention, and personalized financial services transform raw payment data into strategic business intelligence and new revenue streams.
